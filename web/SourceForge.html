Sorce Forge Statement

From afar I've admired Source Forge and watched its progress and growth in services with the intention of one day migrating over. With the sudden closure of libeth's previous home site (thanks while it lasted Netpedia!) the day has come sooner than I had expected. Which isn't necessarily a bad thing, I'm pushed into a bit of updating and clean up work to make the project presentable to a wider audience.

I hope to have all libeth materials fully migrated into the Source Forge realm by mid February. Until then anticipate the hazards of broken and out of date links and information in the content here.

Thank you for your patience.

-Daniel Yacob

Where is LibEth Headed

The original intentions of the 0.34 release are pretty much abandoned at this point and the bulk of which will now be pushed off to 0.35. 0.35 has been in development for sometime, internally it is a different creature from the previous releases, some updates are be backed int the 0.34 tree periodically. My original intention was to move into Source Forge at the 0.35 release. Some forthcoming features are:

v0.35 Targets
  • Extended Ethiopic is handled more efficiently, making libeth leaner, meaner.
  • More fonts faces, character sets (need to get a list together).
  • Memoizing get_fchar to cache codes already converted -speedy!
  • Enhanced locale logic.
  • Calendar code data for as many as 12 languages.
  • Calendar codes have Eritrean holidays.
  • estrftime is a simple wrapper around strftime, Ethiopic % escapes are provided.
  • #defines are name space safe.
  • Maybe Gurage calendar conversions.
v0.36 Targets
  • Definitely Gurage calendar conversions.
  • Introductory indexing features.
  • Alternative coallation systems.
